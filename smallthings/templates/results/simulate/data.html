Prep 20 min
Serves 2-4

2 medium courgettes (yellow and white are perfect for this), finely sliced or shaved with a mandoline
4 radishes, finely sliced or shaved with a mandoline
1 handful pea shoots
1 red chilli, halved, deseeded and finely sliced
Zest and juice of 1 lemon
½ tsp salt
Black pepper
1 bunch mint, leaves picked, large leaves finely sliced
A few sprigs of dill, leaves picked
10g parmesan, finely sliced or peeled with a potato peeler
2 tbsp smoked roast almonds
Lemon juice
Rapeseed oil

Put the courgettes, radishes, pea shoots and chilli in a bowl and season with the lemon zest, salt and pepper.

Add the mint, dill, parmesan and almonds, squeeze over the lemon juice, and drizzle with a little rapeseed oil. You don’t want it greasy or over-dressed, so be light with the oil, but taste until it’s dressed and seasoned to your liking. Pack into a tub.

Prep 20 min
Serves 2-4

450g ripe, room-temperature heirloom tomatoes (a variety of colours, shapes and sizes), washed and chopped or sliced – keep the seeds and juice
1½ tsp sea salt
½ tsp caster sugar
1 tbsp capers, rinsed
½ red onion, finely sliced
1 tbsp red-wine vinegar
2 thick slices stale sourdough, torn into chunks
10g kalamata olives, sliced
A handful of basil, large leaves torn, plus extra for garnish
4 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil

In a bowl, toss the tomatoes in the salt and sugar with the capers and set aside for 10-15 minutes to bring out their juices.

In another bowl, cover the red onion with the vinegar and allow it to sit and mellow.

Toss the sourdough into the tomatoes to soak up the juices, set aside for a couple of minutes, then add the olives and basil, and toss to combine.

Whisk the olive oil into the onion and vinegar and pour over the salad to dress. Garnish with basil leaves and pack.

Prep 30 min
Cook 1 hr 20 min
Serves 6

200g cherry tomatoes (it’s nice to use yellow and red here), halved
Extra-virgin olive oil
Salt and black pepper
1 tbsp fresh rosemary leaves, chopped
1 large pinch saffron
2 tbsp pine nuts
2 shallots, peeled and finely chopped
¼ bulb of fennel, finely chopped
1 red chilli, deseeded and finely chopped
300g orzo
2 tbsp kalamata olives, pitted and torn in half
100g broad beans, blanched and peeled
1 handful parsley, finely chopped
1 handful dill, finely chopped
Juice of ½ lemon

Heat the oven to 160C/320F/gas 2½. Coat the tomato halves in a little olive oil and season well with salt and pepper. Lay out on a baking sheet and scatter over the rosemary. Roast for 45-60 minutes, until softened, shrivelled and concentrated. Meanwhile, soak the saffron in half a tablespoon of boiling water to release its colour and flavour.

Toast the pine nuts in a dry frying pan until golden and nutty – keep a close eye on them, because they can burn when left unattended.

Heat a little olive oil over a medium heat in a frying pan and cook the shallots, fennel and chilli with a pinch of salt and pepper until softened, sweet and starting to caramelise – about five minutes, or until the fennel is taking on some colour. Set aside.

Cook the orzo in plenty of boiling salted water according to packet instructions, or until al dente, then drain and rinse under cold water. Transfer to a bowl and, once cool, pour over a glug of olive oil and the saffron and its soaking liquid, forking through to separate the grains. Stir in the tomatoes, pine nuts, olives, broad beans, herbs and the fennel mixture.

Squeeze over the lemon juice and check the seasoning. Add a glug of extra-virgin olive oil for good measure, then pack.

Prep 20 min
Serves 2

4 baby cucumbers, or one large cucumber
1 big pinch sea salt
1 handful roasted salted peanuts
1 tsp black sesame seeds
½ tsp smashed coriander seeds
1 green jalapeño, halved, deseeded and finely sliced
1 handful coriander leaves, roughly chopped

For the dressing
1½ tsp grated ginger
1 garlic clove, grated
2 tbsp rapeseed oil
1 tsp toasted sesame oil
1 tbsp rice vinegar
1 tsp honey
Zest and juice of ½ lime
1 pinch white pepper

On a chopping board, bash the cucumbers with a rolling pin, then roughly chop them up into 2cm-3cm wide pieces. Put in a bowl and season with salt. Set aside for 10 minutes, then toss with the other salad ingredients.

Whisk together the dressing ingredients and dress. Adjust to taste with a little more sesame oil, lime or salt, and pack.
